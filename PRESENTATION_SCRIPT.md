### Opening (30 seconds)

---

### The Problem (30 seconds)
> "Middle school students often struggle with abstract physics concepts like orbital mechanics and escape velocity. Textbooks make these concepts feel distant and boring. We wanted to change that."

---

### The Solution (1 minute)
> "SABRINA is an interactive web-based simulator where students become astronauts on missions to real exoplanets. They learn by doing - launching rockets, managing fuel, landing safely, and collecting scientific data. But here's what makes it special: we use **real NASA data** and **real techniques** like atmospheric spectroscopy and transit photometry."

---

### Live Demo (3-4 minutes)

**Step 1: Welcome (30 seconds)**
> "First, students enter their name and get a fun space-themed identity - like 'Commander Sarah of the Stars' - making them feel like real astronauts."

**Step 2: Planet Selection (30 seconds)**
> "They choose from three real exoplanets: Proxima Centauri b, TRAPPIST-1e, or Kepler-452b. Each has real parameters from NASA's exoplanet archive."

**Step 3: Launch Phase (45 seconds)**
> "During launch, students set the Thrust-to-Weight Ratio. Notice the visual feedback - the slider changes color based on efficiency. This teaches real rocket physics. If TWR is too low, they can't escape Earth's gravity. Too high, they waste fuel."

**Step 4: Cruise Phase (30 seconds)**
> "In space, they see a real-time trajectory visualization. They make mid-course corrections, learning about inertia and orbital mechanics. Each decision affects fuel consumption."

**Step 5: Landing (30 seconds)**
> "Landing is the hardest part - just like real missions! They must slow down using retro-burns. The velocity indicator shows SAFE, WARNING, or TOO FAST. This teaches precise control."

**Step 6: Data Collection (1 minute)**
> "On the surface, students complete interactive activities. First, atmospheric spectroscopy - they analyze light spectra to identify elements, just like NASA does. Second, transit photometry - they measure light curves to calculate planet size, the same technique Kepler used to discover exoplanets. Both activities teach real science."

**Step 7: Success & Sharing (30 seconds)**
> "When they succeed, they earn achievement badges based on performance - like 'Fuel Master' or 'Speed Demon'. They can share their success with friends, encouraging more students to try."

**Step 8: AI Tutor (30 seconds)**
> "Throughout, SABRINA - our AI tutor - is always available. Students can ask questions and get personalized explanations. Watch: [Ask a question] - it responds with age-appropriate explanations."

---

### Technical Highlights (1 minute)
> "Technically, we built this with Flask for the backend, implementing real physics formulas for orbital mechanics. The frontend uses interactive visualizations with SVG and JavaScript. We integrated Google's Gemini AI for the tutor. Most importantly, we used real exoplanet data from NASA's archives - actual distances, gravity factors, and orbital periods."

---

### Impact & Learning (1 minute)
> "This project teaches real aerospace concepts: Newton's laws, escape velocity, fuel management, and NASA's actual research techniques. It's designed for classrooms but works on any device. Students learn by doing, not just reading. We've seen how achievement badges and sharing create excitement and encourage persistence."

---

### Closing (30 seconds)
> "SABRINA makes space science accessible, engaging, and fun. It combines real NASA data with game mechanics and AI tutoring to create something unique. Thank you, and we're happy to answer questions!"
